How are the score and threshold computed?

Sources are first filtered (digital education, AI, EdTech, policies/standards). Then each item receives a global score (0–100) combining several dimensions (research, policy, institutions, potential impact for teaching).

Weights (normalized) R/P/I/M: —

  • R — Research: articles, journals, conferences, calls for papers, scientific results.
  • P — Policy: laws, regulations, standards, frameworks.
  • I — Institution: major agencies & authorities (UNESCO, OECD, CNIL, NCSC, ministries…).
  • M — Impact: direct impact on teaching, pupils/teachers, EdTech.
